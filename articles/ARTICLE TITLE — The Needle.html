The Schism That Never Ends: How Identity Outlives Ideology

Catholic vs. Protestant. Shia vs. Sunni. The intellectual dispute fades — the identity remains. And with it, the conflict.

Issue 001
Pillar History
Published April 2026
Read time 8 min

Every schism in history follows the same arc. Two people read the same text and disagree about what it means. The disagreement becomes a debate. The debate becomes a movement. The movement fractures. And then, long after anyone can remember exactly what the original argument was about, people are still killing each other over it.

This is not a religious phenomenon. It is a human one. The pattern repeats across theology, politics, and ideology with such regularity that it begins to look less like a coincidence and more like a structural feature of how groups form and maintain themselves.
